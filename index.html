A metaphoric soundscape to experience a Highly Sensitive Person’s hearing

15-20% of the population perceive and feel differently. They are more aware of subtleties but are also more easily overwhelmed. They are called Highly Sensitive Persons. High Sensitivity is a personality trait that is not to be confused with extro-/introversion. Furthermore, every HSP is different. This project focuses on a very small part of High Sensitivity, the perception of hearing. HSPs have a lower threshold of hearing but are also more easily overwhelmed by intense, and loud sounds. This project tries to provide a metaphor for a Highly Sensitive Person’s hearing. This metaphor enables non-HSPs to experience a HSP’s hearing.

What this comparison can tell us

These quotes are taking from a user study of non-HSP listening to those sounds and answering this question: “How did listening to these two soundscapes change your perception of HSPs?”

“[…] I guess my redefinition of Highly Sensitive would […] instead of going for “how you subjectively perceive that” [it would go for] “how you objectively perceive that”, like biologically speaking. […] So, you are the same person, but you are now listening to this in a way different way and therefore you are acting on that in a different way because it is not the same sound. […]”

“[…]. If you actually feel it that way, I mean then it’s something that you have no control over. I guess […]”

“The embodied feeling of it [the sounds] makes you kinda understand a little bit better as opposed to someone telling you these things.”
